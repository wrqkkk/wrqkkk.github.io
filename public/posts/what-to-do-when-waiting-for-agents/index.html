Review Concepts and Theorems used in my courses.#

Concepts in math are tightly woven together: stochastic processes sit at the heart of time series analysis, which also demands a strong command of statistics; real analysis provides much of the rigor needed to make sense of results in stochastic analysis; not to mention how algebra quietly but powerfully runs through nearly every path in mathematics, statistics, and computer science.

It’s been a while since I realized how quickly my understanding of concepts begins to fade once I step away from the steady orbit of study, review, and practice. However, it is hard to find both time in my schedule and mental room in my mind to look back systematically. I have to save my energy for more urgent tasks. So, for now, what I can do is break large review tasks into small, manageable ones and work on them in fragments of spare time.

Write my daily log.#

I really need to build a few small habits to make my life run more smoothly.

Successful samples are shown below:

  • Do not leave any object in an open pocket while riding.
  • Count the number of objects I need when I leave home, and do the same when I return.
  • Automatically place things back after I use them.
  • Connect a new habit to an existing one to minimize the energy cost of maintaining it.

  • Eat and sleep wisely to get more energy.
